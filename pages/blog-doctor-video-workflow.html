The shortest workflow to start making videos as a doctor (quite literally)

Most doctors overthink video creation. Cameras, lights, scripts, editing, how they look, what colleagues will say. That thinking is the real blocker. Not time. Not skill.

Here is the simplest workflow I use. You can copy this exactly and start today.

Perfection is the enemy of good. And in healthcare marketing, like it or not, jo dikhta hai woh bikta hai.

Yes, watching yourself on camera can feel embarrassing. Yes, your first few videos will feel awkward. That is normal. Avoiding it does not make it go away. Doing it repeatedly does.

  1. Create a fresh Instagram account (important)

    Use a fresh phone or a device where you have never logged into Instagram. Create a new Instagram account only for your medical content.

    Why this matters: Your first videos will not immediately reach friends, relatives, or people you know in real life. This removes the embarrassment barrier. You are more likely to post consistently when you are not worried about judgement.

  2. Generate video ideas using AI (do not brainstorm manually)

    Do not sit and think, â€œWhat should I post?â€ Use a simple prompt like this:

    You are a medical content assistant for a [your specialty] doctor. Search widely and come up with trendy, patient-friendly video ideas that help educate people and slowly build trust. These should not be ads. The goal is to attract patients or health-interested people.

    You will get a list of ideas. Pick one. Do not optimise. Do not compare. Just pick.

  3. Turn the idea into a script (high retention, simple language)

    Now take that idea and use another prompt:

    You are a medical content strategist and assistant for a [your specialty] doctor.

    Your goal is to turn the following idea into a high-impact, short-form video script (under 60 seconds) for Instagram/Shorts. Use the 'Hook-Value-CTA' framework.

    Hook: Start with a strong, curiosity-inducing hook that addresses a common patient pain point or myth.
    Value: Explain the concept in 3-4 simple sentences. Use analogies instead of medical jargon. Focus on what the patient needs to know.
    Style: Keep the language conversational, like a doctor talking to a patient in an OPD, not a classroom lecture.
    CTA: End with a simple call to action like "Follow for more [specialty] tips" or "Share this with someone who needs it."

    Topic: [paste your idea here]

    Read the script once. Make small additions in your own words if you want. Do not rewrite the entire thing.

  4. Record using a teleprompter (no memorising)

    Download any teleprompter app. Paste the script. Place the phone at eye level. Hit record. Read it out.

Time reality check

Once you get used to this workflow:

  • Idea + script: 5 minutes
  • Recording: 5 minutes
  • Editing or upload: 10 minutes

20 minutes a day. Thatâ€™s it. Consistency beats everything here. Not camera quality. Not confidence. Not editing skills.

Final reminder

You will cringe at your own videos initially. Everyone does. You will feel exposed. That does not mean you should stop.

Doctors who win online are not better speakers. They just started earlier and stayed longer.
